Week 7: Layouts



During this week, we were introduced to layouts, the concept and its rules.


To exercise our capability of using layouts correctly by dividing in teams and shaping a two-page part of a magazine on paper, with cutouts of differently colored paper that signified different rules/ categories that had to be included, such as “author 1 picture”, “author 2 picture”, “Ad 1”, “Ad 2”, etc.


We came up with a back story for our magazine and made it to be a National Geographic kids section about the South and the North pole, written by two brothers.


We started with a prototype of how we want our work to look like, as seen on the right.

The work we did together went very well, with everyone contributing towards our end goal.


The feedback we got from Josh and our classmates was very positive, with the exception that the pictures should have their main focus further away from the middle of the pages, as that wouldn’t fully show when bending the pages inwards.

My logo and its story

 
 

When creating my logo, I thought about who I am as a person. And according to MBTI 16 personalities, I am an ETNJ, which means I am "a commander: bold, imaginative and strong-willed, always finding a way".

Besides ICT as a field of work being good for me, other options would be becoming a lawyer or an entrepreneur, which made me want to build a logo with an allure of seriousness, as if I would use it to represent myself in a competitive field of work. You can see a mock-up of a business card on the right.
