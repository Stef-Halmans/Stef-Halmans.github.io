Video production

 

The team and the task

Our team is composed of me, Martin, Maryiana, Maud, and Zhan/Jan. We collaborated very well through the entire time.

 

We started our work by brainstorming for an idea. We decided to settle for a story type of video, and decided to make it a funny one to attract the viewers, so we started brainstorming.

 

This leads us to our story. A homeless man from Eindhoven is asked about his life pro tip, and he suggests always having a pen. His story begins when he was a young boy and lost his pen in school. He couldn’t take notes, and didn’t know when his exams were coming. Because of that, he couldn’t pass, and with no graduation diploma, he didn’t succeed in getting a job. He tried to exchange his jewelry and other goods for food, but that didn’t last for long. Everyone else around him was making moves in life, starting families, but not him. He was so skinny that he wasn’t only getting rejected, but he also got ill. And this is how he ended up being homeless.

 

We divded tasks evenly. I wrote the scripts, was the director, reviewed the storyboards and editing, and acted as the interviewer.

Martin had the main role of being a homeless man and helped editing.

Mariyana was responsible for the technical arrangements, from camera to microphone. She was my co-director too, and helped Martin edit the footage, after acting as the girl rejecting the man.

Zhan acted as the dealer and helped with technical parts, together with Maud.

 

For processing the video, the software used was Da Vinci Resolve. All the music is non-copyrighted and sourced from Pixabay.

Storyboard task

We were assigned into teams and tasked to come up with 3 to 4 post-its describing interconnected actions that could be switched between each other and still make sense. I believe we have achieved a good result, as we presented it in front of the class and they were interexchangeable.

What I learned

I believe this exercise trained us for the actual task of storyboarding, which we would later use for our videography assignment.

When assembling a (short) movie, we are sometimes required to alter the way in which our scenes are ordered, while maintaining the same plot, for the sake of maintaining the emotional value that the movie brings to the viewer.

Layouts



During this week, we were introduced to layouts, the concept and its rules.


To exercise our capability of using layouts correctly by dividing in teams and shaping a two-page part of a magazine on paper, with cutouts of differently colored paper that signified different rules/ categories that had to be included, such as “author 1 picture”, “author 2 picture”, “Ad 1”, “Ad 2”, etc.


We came up with a back story for our magazine and made it to be a National Geographic kids section about the South and the North pole, written by two brothers.


We started with a prototype of how we want our work to look like, as seen on the right.

The work we did together went very well, with everyone contributing towards our end goal.


The feedback we got from Josh and our classmates was very positive, with the exception that the pictures should have their main focus further away from the middle of the pages, as that wouldn’t fully show when bending the pages inwards.
